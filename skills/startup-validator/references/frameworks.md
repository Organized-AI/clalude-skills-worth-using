# M&A Validation Frameworks

## Framework 1: Acquisition Target Assessment

### Market Opportunity (25 points)

#### TAM Analysis (10 points)
- **10 pts**: TAM >$10B with clear path to capture
- **7 pts**: TAM $1-10B with defined strategy
- **4 pts**: TAM $500M-$1B, niche opportunity
- **1 pt**: TAM <$500M, limited growth potential

#### Market Growth (8 points)
- **8 pts**: Market growing >25% YoY
- **6 pts**: Market growing 15-25% YoY
- **4 pts**: Market growing 5-15% YoY
- **2 pts**: Market growing <5% YoY or declining

#### Competitive Position (7 points)
- **7 pts**: Clear market leader or unique positioning
- **5 pts**: Strong competitor with differentiation
- **3 pts**: Mid-tier player, some differentiation
- **1 pt**: Undifferentiated, crowded market

### Financial Health (25 points)

#### Revenue Metrics (10 points)
- **10 pts**: ARR >$10M, growth >100% YoY
- **7 pts**: ARR $5-10M, growth >50% YoY
- **4 pts**: ARR $1-5M, growth >25% YoY
- **1 pt**: ARR <$1M or declining

#### Profitability (8 points)
- **8 pts**: Gross margin >75%, profitable or clear path
- **6 pts**: Gross margin 60-75%, approaching profitability
- **4 pts**: Gross margin 45-60%, 2+ years to profitability
- **2 pts**: Gross margin <45%, unclear path

#### Unit Economics (7 points)
- **7 pts**: LTV/CAC >5x, payback <6 months
- **5 pts**: LTV/CAC 3-5x, payback 6-12 months
- **3 pts**: LTV/CAC 2-3x, payback 12-18 months
- **1 pt**: LTV/CAC <2x or payback >18 months

### Strategic Fit (25 points)

#### Synergy Potential (10 points)
- **10 pts**: >$10M identified synergies, high probability
- **7 pts**: $5-10M synergies, medium-high probability
- **4 pts**: $2-5M synergies, medium probability
- **1 pt**: <$2M synergies or unclear

#### Customer Overlap (8 points)
- **8 pts**: >30% customer overlap, strong cross-sell
- **6 pts**: 15-30% overlap, clear cross-sell opportunity
- **4 pts**: 5-15% overlap, some potential
- **2 pts**: <5% overlap, minimal synergy

#### Technology Compatibility (7 points)
- **7 pts**: Tech stack highly compatible, easy integration
- **5 pts**: Compatible with minor adjustments
- **3 pts**: Some compatibility challenges
- **1 pt**: Major integration challenges

### Team & Execution (25 points)

#### Founder Experience (10 points)
- **10 pts**: Experienced founders, previous exits
- **7 pts**: Strong domain expertise, proven track record
- **4 pts**: Some relevant experience
- **1 pt**: First-time founders, limited experience

#### Team Quality (8 points)
- **8 pts**: Complete team, all key roles filled, low attrition
- **6 pts**: Strong team, most roles filled, <10% attrition
- **4 pts**: Adequate team, some gaps, 10-20% attrition
- **2 pts**: Weak team, major gaps, >20% attrition

#### Execution Track Record (7 points)
- **7 pts**: Consistently exceeds goals, strong execution
- **5 pts**: Generally meets goals, good execution
- **3 pts**: Mixed results, execution concerns
- **1 pt**: Poor execution, misses targets

## Framework 2: Investment Due Diligence

### Financial Due Diligence Checklist

#### Revenue Validation
- [ ] 3 years of audited financials
- [ ] Revenue recognition policies reviewed
- [ ] Customer contracts validated
- [ ] Revenue concentration analysis
- [ ] Churn/retention rates verified

#### Cost Structure
- [ ] COGS breakdown and trends
- [ ] Operating expense analysis
- [ ] Working capital requirements
- [ ] Capital expenditure plans
- [ ] Debt schedule and covenants

#### Projections
- [ ] Financial model review
- [ ] Assumption validation
- [ ] Sensitivity analysis
- [ ] Scenario planning
- [ ] Cash flow projections

### Operational Due Diligence

#### Management Assessment
- [ ] Management interviews conducted
- [ ] Organizational structure reviewed
- [ ] Key person dependencies identified
- [ ] Succession planning evaluated
- [ ] Compensation structure assessed

#### Customer Analysis
- [ ] Top 20 customers validated
- [ ] Customer satisfaction surveys
- [ ] Reference calls completed
- [ ] Contract terms reviewed
- [ ] Renewal rates analyzed

#### Technology Assessment
- [ ] Architecture review completed
- [ ] Code quality assessment
- [ ] Technical debt identified
- [ ] Security audit conducted
- [ ] Scalability evaluation

## Framework 3: Risk Assessment Matrix

### Critical Risks (Stop/Investigate)
- Legal issues or pending litigation
- Undisclosed liabilities >$500K
- Key customer concentration >50%
- Founder departures announced
- Technical architecture red flags

### High Risks (Detailed Investigation)
- Revenue decline >10% QoQ
- Churn rate >15% annually
- Negative unit economics
- Significant technical debt
- Cultural misalignment

### Medium Risks (Standard DD)
- Revenue growth <industry average
- Moderate customer concentration
- Some team turnover
- Minor technical issues
- Integration complexity

### Low Risks (Manageable)
- Strong fundamentals across dimensions
- Minor operational improvements needed
- Standard integration challenges
- Manageable technical updates

## Usage Guidelines

### Scoring Approach
1. Evaluate each dimension independently
2. Use sub-category scoring guides
3. Document rationale for each score
4. Aggregate to overall score
5. Interpret against thresholds

### Red Flag Protocol
- Identify any critical risks immediately
- Document all concerns clearly
- Provide mitigation recommendations
- Adjust scores accordingly

### Recommendation Framework
- 90-100: Strong BUY - Move quickly, competitive situation
- 75-89: BUY - Proceed with standard DD
- 60-74: HOLD - Deep investigation required
- <60: PASS - Unless extraordinary circumstances
